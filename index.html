Data-to-decision pathway

Evidence before conclusions.

A traceable route from an engineering question to a deployable decision.

  1. 01
    Define the question

    Specify the physics, constraints, required decision and success criteria.

  2. 02
    Acquire & qualify the evidence

    Select and acquire signals, images and test data; assess calibration, traceability, representativeness and uncertainty.

  3. 03
    Validate the model & interpretation

    Test the model and interpretation against qualified evidence, explicitly checking stated limits and relevant failure modes.

  4. 04
    Decide, deploy & monitor

    Translate the result into a decision, tool or workflow with stated uncertainty and a feedback loop.

Why are Steps 02 and 03 separate gates? Step 02 asks whether the evidence is fit for the intended decision; Step 03 asks whether the model and interpretation are supported by that qualified evidence. If either gate fails, return upstream before deployment.

About

From first principles to real-world machines and intelligent systems.

My work sits where mathematical modelling meets physical machines: thermodynamics, aerodynamics, vibration, measurement, mechanical design and intelligent computation.

I began in aerospace engineering and UAV aerodynamics, translating CFD, propeller–motor matching and structural-vibration analysis into decisions for real aircraft. At Cranfield I developed gas-turbine performance and diagnostics models; at Politecnico di Milano I built measurement systems for rotating machinery; and at Siemens Energy I apply the same physics-led approach to gas-turbine R&D, industrial imaging and deployable AI/ML.

The common thread is not a particular software stack. It is the ability to move between equations, experiments, machines and implementation—and to understand where each can fail.
