Data-to-decision pathway

Evidence before conclusions.

A traceable route from an engineering question to a deployable decision.

  1. 01
    Define the question

    Specify the physics, constraints, required decision and success criteria.

  2. 02
    Acquire & qualify the evidence

    Select and acquire signals, images and test data; assess calibration, traceability, representativeness and uncertainty.

  3. 03
    Validate the model & interpretation

    Test the model and interpretation against qualified evidence, explicitly checking stated limits and relevant failure modes.

  4. 04
    Decide, deploy & monitor

    Translate the result into a decision, tool or workflow with stated uncertainty and a feedback loop.

Why two gates? High-quality data can still be interpreted by the wrong model; a plausible model is not defensible when the underlying evidence is weak. Both gates must pass before deployment.

If either gate fails, return to the question, evidence or model before acting.

01 / About

Engineering across physics, measurement and software.

My career has followed one consistent question: how can difficult physical phenomena be converted into reliable engineering evidence?

I began in aerospace engineering and UAV aerodynamics, developed gas-turbine performance and diagnostics software at Cranfield University, and built wireless measurement systems for rotating machinery at Politecnico di Milano. Today, at Siemens Energy, I contribute to industrial gas-turbine R&D through digital methods, data-informed analysis and advanced measurement.

I enjoy work that connects a physical problem to a repeatable experiment, a defensible model and, ultimately, a tool that engineers can trust.

Beyond engineering

The same curiosity, in a different landscape.

Cycling gives me space to reset, observe and keep moving through difficult terrain. I also enjoy RC aircraft modelling and tennis—interests that have kept experimentation, motion and practical problem-solving close throughout my career.
