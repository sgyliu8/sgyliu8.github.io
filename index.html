Profile

Whole-engine depth. Cross-disciplinary reach.

My core discipline is gas-turbine whole-engine performance: understanding how compressors, combustors, turbines, controls and operating constraints interact across design and off-design conditions, and using that understanding to form whole-engine technical judgements.

I combine thermodynamics, first-principles physics and applied mathematics with real-machine testing, advanced measurement and model–test correlation. My current R&D extends this foundation into industrial imaging and AI/ML, from model architecture and algorithm development through to validation and engineering deployment.

Across both areas, the common objective is to define technical roadmaps, qualify evidence, resolve cross-disciplinary risks and translate complex analysis into decisions that can be implemented, traced and reviewed.

Decision Framework

How I turn evidence into engineering decisions.

A traceable route from a clearly defined engineering question to a qualified, deployable and reviewable decision.

  1. 01 Define the question

    Specify the physics, operating constraints, required decision and acceptance criteria.

  2. 02 Acquire and qualify the evidence

    Acquire performance maps, operating data, signals, images and test conditions; assess calibration, traceability, representativeness and uncertainty.

  3. 03 Validate the model and interpretation

    Test physics-based and AI/ML models and their interpretation against qualified evidence, stated limits and relevant failure modes.

  4. 04 Decide, deploy and monitor

    Translate the result into a decision, tool or workflow with stated uncertainty, traceability and a feedback loop.

Why are Steps 02 and 03 separate gates? Step 02 asks whether the evidence is fit for the intended decision; Step 03 asks whether the model and interpretation are supported by that qualified evidence. If either gate fails, return upstream before deployment.
