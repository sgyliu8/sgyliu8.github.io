Profile

System-level depth. Cross-disciplinary reach.

My core discipline is gas-turbine engine performance and systems integration: understanding how compressors, combustors, turbines, controls and operating constraints interact across design and off-design conditions.

I combine thermodynamics, first-principles physics and applied mathematics with real-machine testing, advanced measurement and model–test correlation. My current R&D extends this foundation into industrial imaging and AI/ML, from model architecture and algorithm development through to validation and engineering deployment.

Across both areas, the common objective is to define technical roadmaps, qualify evidence, resolve cross-disciplinary risks and translate complex analysis into decisions that can be implemented, traced and reviewed.

Decision Framework

How I turn evidence into engineering decisions.

A traceable route from a clearly defined engineering question to a qualified, deployable and reviewable decision.

  1. Define the question

    Set the physics, operating conditions, constraints, decision and acceptance criteria.

  2. Qualify the evidence

    Assess calibration, test conditions, traceability, representativeness and uncertainty.

  3. Validate the model

    Test the model and its interpretation against qualified evidence, limits and relevant failure modes.

  4. Decide, deploy and monitor

    Translate the result into a decision, tool or workflow with stated uncertainty and monitoring.

Two independent engineering checks

Evidence quality Is the evidence fit for this decision?

Check calibration, traceability, representativeness and uncertainty.

Model validity Is the model supported by qualified evidence?

Check correlation, limits, interpretation and relevant failure modes.

If either check fails, return to the relevant upstream stage before deployment.
